ROME

Rome is the capital city and a special comune of Italy, as well as the capital of the Lazio region.
The city has been a major human settlement for almost three millennia.

Attractions in Abruzzo

Parco Nazionale d’Abruzzo,
Abruzzo

The Parco Nazionale d’Abruzzo was established in 1923 to protect nature and to save certain wild animals from extinction. It’s in the heart of the central Apennines between the Abruzzi, Lazio and Molise and its 50,000+ hectares are home to 25 towns.

Read More

Lake Scanno,
Abruzzo

Located in the Province of L'Aquila, Lake Scanno is known as the heart of Italy due to its distinctive shape. The traditional village of Scanno is located on its northern edge. The Sagittario flows out of the lake towards the north.

Read More

Castle of Roccascalegna,
Abruzzo

Located in the province of Chieti, Castle of Roccascalegna was principally constructed in the 15th and 16th centuries, at a time of military rivalry between the Angevin and Aragonese armies. The castle was featured as a location in the film Tale of Tales.

SICILY

The Mediterranean's biggest island is separated from the mainland by the Strait of Messina.
A mountainous spot, Sicily's coast and its small islands sit at the foot of volcanoes, including Etna, Stromboli and Vulcano.

The Greeks, Romans, Normans and Catalans all left their mark on the island in the form of Byzantine palaces,
Gothic castles and Baroque flourishes in capital Palermo. Most larger towns offer interesting museums, but Etna
and the Hellenic temples in Agrigento offer particularly unique sights.
